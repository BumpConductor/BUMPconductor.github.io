BumpConductor is a system that transforms complex problems into effective solutions.

Why is it needed?

The World Economic Forum (WEF), in its 2016 Future of Jobs report, states that "a Fourth Industrial Revolution is interacting with other socio-economic and demographic factors to create a perfect storm of business model change in all industries, resulting in major disruptions to labour markets."

Furthermore it lists "complex problem solving" as the No.1 skillset required to support this new situation. But our independent research has highlighted that individuals and organizations are completely lacking complex problem solving capabilites in almost every area!

That is why we created the bumpconductor concept. A highly scalable system that helps individuals and organisations transform complex probems into effective solutions.

What is the BUMPConductor System?

The BumpConductor® system consists of a framework, method, and a technology platform. The foundations for the BumpConductor is our theory of BUMPS. Our BUMPS theory advocates that we can better solve problems by visualising complex problems in problem landscape.

What are BUMPS?

BUMPS® is a metaphor we use it in our advance problem solving techniques. BUMPS stands for 'Big Unsolved Meaningful Problems worth Solving'.

Our BUMPS based approach can help teams think about problems in a radically different way. Using this as a foundation we can better visualise, analyse, and communicate complex problem scenarios.

Techniques

The tools to get things done

Most problem solving techniques are designed to solve problems in isolation and are often used at tactical levels e.g. lean process improvements. We think the time is right for a more ambitious approach. Our unique BUMPS based framework links strategy with execution and helps align an organisation. It covers four areas:

  • Destination - Where you want to go
  • Purpose - Why you are going there
  • Course - How you're going to get there
  • Navigation - What to do about the challenges on the way

Network

Collaborative Problem Solving

In this age of globalisation we need to collaborate as well as we complete. When encountering BUMPS we see five choices:
